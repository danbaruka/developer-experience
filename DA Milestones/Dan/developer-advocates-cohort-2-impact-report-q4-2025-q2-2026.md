## Executive Summary

From **Q4 2025 through Q2 2026**, the **Developer Advocates Cohort 2** operated as Intersect's frontline program for developer enablement on Cardano. Cohort 2 translated Open Source Strategy priorities into practical action: onboarding, education, community engagement, documentation, and feedback loops that help the ecosystem improve continuously.

**The sentiment across the period has been strongly positive.** Participants showed growing confidence, rising participation in working groups, and genuine enthusiasm for building on Cardano. The program's role has been to channel that optimism into structured support so more builders can move from interest to contribution to sustained impact.

Key outcomes include the launch of [devex.intersectmbo.org](https://devex.intersectmbo.org), a dedicated [Developer Advocate blog page](https://devcommunity.io/pages/developeradvocate) on DevCommunity.io, **30+ DevEx Working Group sessions**, multilingual contributor workshops, strategic partnerships across East Africa, and a **70-response developer experience survey** that now guides improvement priorities. Challenges remain around onboarding clarity and documentation consistency, but Cohort 2 has built momentum, trust, and a repeatable model for developer advocacy that the ecosystem can scale.

## Relationship with the Intersect Open Source Office

The Developer Advocates Cohort 2 is a **program of the Intersect Open Source Office (OSO)**, operating in close alignment with the **Open Source Committee (OSC)**. This relationship defines how advocacy connects to governance, funding, and long-term open-source strategy.

| Function | OSO / OSC role | Developer Advocates Cohort 2 role |
| -------- | -------------- | --------------------------------- |
| **Strategy** | Sets Open Source Strategy and committee direction | Translates strategy into developer-facing programs and feedback |
| **Governance** | OSC reviews progress and milestone delivery | Submits shared DevEx reports and survey insights to OSC |
| **Funding &amp; programs** | Evolves maintainer, contributor, and ecosystem programs | Surfaces developer needs to inform OSO program design |
| **Community bridge** | OSO contact point for open-source participation | Onboards developers into Intersect, OSC, and working groups |
| **Documentation &amp; DevEx** | Supports ecosystem-wide open-source health | Owns DevEx WG sessions, hub content, and improvement proposals |
| **Project support** | OSO Project Support for complex technical consultations | Connects builders to OSO services and ecosystem resources |

Cohort 2 does not operate in isolation. It is the **human layer** of Intersect's open-source mission: listening to developers, reducing friction, and returning structured insight so OSO and OSC decisions reflect real builder experience. Milestone reporting, survey analysis, pain-point documentation, and working group session plans all feed this loop.

## Intersect Developer Experience Survey 2025-26: Summary

Cohort 2 collected **70 responses** across English, French, and other language editions of the **Intersect Developer Experience Survey 2025-26**. Developers reported overall experience around **3.3/5**, but **motivation to keep contributing at ~4.3/5**; top challenges were getting started, tooling and documentation, and learning core concepts, while **community** remained the most cited ecosystem strength and **44%** asked for materials in other languages. These findings are shaping Cohort 2 priorities on onboarding, mentorship, localization, and clearer paid contribution pathways. **The full survey report will be attached to this post when available.**

## Lessons Learned and Areas for Growth

| Theme | Lesson | Direction |
| ----- | ------ | --------- |
| Onboarding | Content remains fragmented across repos and platforms | Canonical roadmap and profile-based entry points |
| Documentation | Quality and freshness vary widely | Standards, ownership, and review cadence |
| Developer support | Highest demand, lowest rated availability | Scale clinics, async guides, and peer mentorship |
| Localization | 44% want materials in other languages | Glossary, translations, localized sessions |
| Economics | Paid OSS opportunities are hard to find | Connect onboarding to funding and employment paths |
| Hybrid delivery | Virtual scales; in-person converts strongly | Invest in regional hubs alongside global sessions |

These are not setbacks. They are **shared learning** that keeps the program honest, responsive, and aligned with what developers actually need.


## Looking Ahead

The Developer Advocates Cohort 2 enters the next phase with proven formats, strong community sentiment, and clear improvement targets.

**Near term (Q3 2026):** Publish the unified onboarding roadmap, tooling decision guide, and default developer environment recommendation; pilot localized sessions; track survey metrics quarter over quarter.

**Medium term:** Deepen university and hub partnerships, expand project spotlights, strengthen collaboration with OSO Project Support and technical working groups, and grow the DevEx hub as the ecosystem's trusted developer front door.

The Cardano ecosystem's greatest strength remains its **community**: generous, curious builders who help each other persist. Cohort 2 exists to make that strength **structured, repeatable, and welcoming** through collective practice under the Intersect Open Source Office.

From Q4's foundational launch through Q1's deepening engagement to Q2's application focus, the trajectory is clear. **Momentum is real. Sentiment is positive. Improvement is continuous.** The capacity to solve developer experience challenges together is stronger today than it was nine months ago.
